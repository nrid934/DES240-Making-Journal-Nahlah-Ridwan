## Independent Data Portrait

### Chosen topic: Iftar Night Meals

### Collecting data by hand

Using a small notebook, I noted down the meals I had for "iftar", five days in a row. In my day-to-day life, I tend not to pay attention to what I have eaten unless I really enjoyed it or if it was something new. This is especially seen in the context of Ramadan where I tend to forget memorable meals shared with friends by the time the next Ramadan has arrived. 

### Step 3: Designing my visualisation 

![alt text](<../assets/week-01/Week 1/Untitled_Artwork (2).png>)

*iftar has a major occurence during the holy month of Ramadan. Because there are so many of them (30 maximum), it can be difficult to remember what was eaten by the time the next Ramadan arrives. I created this piece to visualise my iftar experiences for 5 evenings in a row.

### Reflection

I chose to track the meals I was having for Iftar (the dinner meal typically used to break fasts in Ramadan) over the course of 5 nights. The reason why I decided to track this data was because after Ramadan, I tend to forget what I spent time eating with my friends and family. Collecting this data and presenting it in such a way that would allow me to better remember the memories of this Ramadan 2026.

When collecting information, I tend to use the 'Notes' app on my phone, thus using a notebook was out of my usual comfort zone. Using a note pad allowed me to just as easily be able to use other forms of communication like drawings and other headings (which can be used on the Notes app, however require a couple extra steps). 

I noticed the spread of cousines, I also noticed that I was not bound to one friend group to spend time with, but rather multiple as Ramadan can also involve spending time with friends and family, the joyful atmosphere usually makes me want to be around other people more than my usual few friends.

When I collected my data, the main aspect that I kept consistent was to ensure that usual food review components were involved like a rating from 1-10. I also chose to represent the food eaten through drawings as a visual indicator of what I had eaten. I am primarily a visual learner and rememberer, thus I chose to use visual methods to convey the look of the plates I had each night, paired with the writing that framed around it.

This relates to data humanism as it takes a normal occurence like a shared meal, and changes the data to more precisely represent the nature of humans, perhaps in a certain way like the tasks included in a to-do list.
